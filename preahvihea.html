WELCOME TO PREAH VIHEAR TEMPLE

Preah Vihear Temple, an awe-inspiring UNESCO World Heritage Site, stands as a testament to Khmer architectural mastery and spiritual devotion. Perched atop a 1,722-foot cliff in the Dângrêk Mountains of Cambodia, this majestic temple complex commands breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Built between the 9th and 12th centuries during the reigns of various Khmer kings, Preah Vihear is dedicated to the Hindu god Shiva, though it also incorporates elements of Buddhist symbolism. Its intricate carvings, elegant colonnades, and series of terraces exemplify the grandeur of Khmer artistry. Despite the challenges posed by its remote location and the border dispute with Thailand, Preah Vihear remains a symbol of resilience and cultural heritage, drawing visitors from around the world to marvel at its timeless beauty and historical significance.
